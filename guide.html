Confidence

Confidence can refer to a general sense of belief and trust in your own ability to control your
life or it might be more situation-specific. For example, you might have high self-confidence
in an area of expertise but feel less confident in other areas. Having a healthy level of
self-confidence can help you become more successful in your personal and professional life.

Critical Thinking

Critical thinking is the intellectually disciplined process of actively and skillfully conceptualizing,
applying, analyzing, synthesizing, and/or evaluating information gathered from, or generated by
observation, experience, reflection, reasoning, or communication, as a guide to belief and action.
In its exemplary form, it is based on universal intellectual values that transcend subject matter
divisions: clarity, accuracy, precision, consistency, relevance, and sound.

Public Speaking

Whether we're talking in a team meeting or presenting in front of an audience,
we all have to speak in public from time to time. We can do this well or we
can do this badly, and the outcome strongly affects the way that people think
about us. This is why public speaking causes so much anxiety and concern.
The good news is that, with thorough preparation and practice
you can overcome your nervousness and perform exceptionally well.
